Timeframes

Week 1 Brainstorm as a group to come up with a group name and decide what we were doing for the project. We compared our skills and allocated different roles for further research to find potential project ideas. Everyone contributed to writing up a personal profile to gain more insight of each group member. Assignment tasks were delegated as everyone had their own respective segments to complete for the week. Ashwathy: Ideal Job’s segment IT Technologies: Brandon, Kien, Kevin and Joseph Industry Data: Sue, Ashwathy Daniel: IT Interview with professional Everyone: Brainstorm ideas for a group name, project idea, complete personal profiles and add their Ideal Job’s to the assignment.
Week 2 Decided on a team name and a project idea. Respective parts of the assignment 2 were being finalized, completed and moved onto GitHub. Focus is now on the project idea, everyone has to think of ideas for the application name and features that can be implemented onto the application. Research was also done by all members of ways to promote sustainability, healthy eating and to gain a better understanding of the 7R’s.
Week 3 Data gathered from the research was collected and ideas discussed. Key features were brainstormed by all group members. An application name was decided on. Ideas for an application name were discussed, and required more time to brainstorm. Potential features of the application were discussed and the purpose of the application was being decided. Further research was conducted to find potential users of the application, the group was split, half the members had begun conducting user research while the other half began researching the necessary tools required to create an application concurrently everyone was contributing to finalize assignment 2 and reviewing all segments then moving them to Github.
Week 4 Assignment 3 is underway, a decision to change the website layout for the assignment was made. For everyone to gain a better insight into the project idea, the vision for the application was established and key features established. A discussion of what we envision our final product. Different segments of the project were delegated by ability. Members with excellent research abilities had continued to fine tune other features that could be implemented. Those with exceptional planning abilities had begun planning the adequate steps required to reach our end goal. A timeframe was set in which we would like to see the project progress.
Week 5 As more research was conducted for the application, it was found many aspects were still lacking. The team had begun designing a logo for the application. Everyone has been informed of how to access the prototype and shown how to operate Figma. Focus is now shifted on the creation of the prototype. Research for the project is progressing and further features were discussed. The scope of the project and where we wanted to be was established. Everyone is continuing working on their parts, however through the nature of the project required collaboration with various team members. While everyone had a “segment” assistance from other members was crucial as the different fields overlapped.
Week 6 The general layout of the application is now completed and a visual prototype is available. A logo for the application was agreed on. The project idea was being improved and more brainstorming was required after some feedback was received. All aspects of the project are finalized, and all relevant data are obtained. The team was split once again into individuals creating who are to create the report and continuation of the prototype creation. The key components of the application can now be tested and a working prototype has been made.
Week 7 Finalization of the prototype, all members are to test the features and give feedback on the design and processes of the application. After discussion and feedback, further design and changes might be required for the application. Begin to conduct usability testing, by finding potential users for trial of the application. All group members will be required to interview different potential users and surveys will be conducted to find issues within the application and its design. The report has been completed and ready to pitch to potential shareholders/collaborators. A graphic designer will be sought after to review the application design/layout. Begin searching for a software engineer who can begin the creation of the application, and also a business manager who understands how to market out application.
Week 8 User testing will still be conducted to get a deeper understanding of how the application is working. Further trials of the application will take place, potential users will be testing the application prototype in a controlled experiment to gain more insights. Different graphic designers and software engineers found will be compared and agreed upon by the team.
Week 9 All data obtained through the user testing will be analysed and briefed. The feedback will be considered and necessary changes are discussed with team members. Feedback from the graphics designer is taken into account and new application layouts are decided on. The prototype will be modified and fine tuned accordingly. Further features will be discussed and decided on whether to include them within the application.
Week 10 The software engineer will be informed of what is required for the application. Business managers will be consulted with the vision of the application and ideas of how the application will be marketed will be discussed. A cybersecurity expert will be chosen and requirements will be discussed. There will be a shift in focus towards marketing. Ideas will be brainstormed and further research will begin in ways of how we want to present the application and what it has to offer.
Week 11 The creation of the application is underway. Weekly meetings will be conducted with the software engineer and cybersecurity experts to discuss issues encountered and new ideas that are presented. A plan will be established with the business manager of how the application will be marketed and sold as. Members will all focus on searching for chefs, bloggers and influencers that might use our application.
Week 12 Development of the application is continuing. Further user testing of the prototype is being conducted. Instead of testing the overall layout and the general overview of the application, testing will now be done on specific features of the application. All features will be further researched and ideas to improve them will be discussed.
Week 13 Results from the user testing are collected and analysed. Collaboration with the software engineer will be done for improvements and adjustment of application features. Bloggers, influencers and chefs are approached and informed of the application vision and what it has to offer. Collecting a pool of people who would like to get involved and promote the application.
Week 14 A working beta version of the application is ready. Members will now work on a plan of how testing will be conducted and different test methods that will be used to test our application. Members will search for a new group of individuals to conduct the application tests. While others continue with the research on ways of marketing the application. Members will also begin creating a new report and a video presentation of the application for future shareholders and investors.
Week 15 A plan for testing will be finalized, application testers are contacted and testing the beta application will begin. Focus groups are formed to gain more insights on how the application operates, Controlled experiments will be conducted alongside a small release for beta testers to use and apply in an environment in which the application will be used. Testers will be asked to provide feedback and log their interactions with the application. Other team members will continue with the liaising between potential investors and the project will be presented.
